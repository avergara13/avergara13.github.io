Architecture and operations case study

Loft OS

A governed operating model for moving AI-assisted work from intake to verified closeout. The design treats scope, authority, evidence, and recoverability as product requirements—not administrative afterthoughts.

Sanitized by design

Public pattern, private implementation.

01 · Product workflow

A workflow that knows when to stop.

The sequence separates observation, assisted work, and human judgment. Each stage produces usable context for the next.

01

Intake & scope

Capture the request, owner, outcome, allowed surfaces, and red lines.

02

Execute

Change only the authorized, traceable surfaces.

03

Review

Inspect behavior, quality, and scope compliance.

04

Authorize

Keep high-impact decisions under explicit human control.

05

Close out

Freeze evidence, reconcile state, and confirm ownership.

02 · Control design

Governance expressed as usable product behavior.

Each control answers a delivery question: what may change, who may approve it, and what proves the result.

01

Scope boundary

  • Named surfaces
  • Explicit acceptance criteria
  • Clear red lines
02

Authority boundary

  • Clear roles
  • Human approval for material release
  • Custody rules for handoffs
03

Evidence boundary

  • Preflight state checks
  • Repeatable verification
  • Known-good closeout

03 · Public boundary

What this portfolio shows—and protects.

The case study explains the operating pattern without exposing private systems, repository details, identifiers, or sensitive implementation evidence.

Shown

  • Workflow stages and role separation
  • Approval, verification, and repair patterns
  • How operational risk maps to controls
  • Employer-relevant systems thinking

Withheld

  • Private source and repository details
  • Live infrastructure and internal links
  • Secrets, security posture, and customer data
  • Internal identifiers and release evidence
